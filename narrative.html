Georgia Housing Story

Back to charts | Dashboard view

Over the past 25 years, Georgia’s housing market has seen both turbulence and remarkable growth. Beginning in 2000, home values were relatively modest, reflecting a steady, predictable climb. But the landscape shifted dramatically with the 2008 financial crisis, which led to a dip in prices that bottomed around 2010.

The Dip & Recovery

The post-2008 crash pushed Georgia’s median home value downward. However, starting in the mid-2010s, the market stabilized and began climbing again, driven by job growth, migration, and economic recovery. By 2020, values were surging, and by 2025, the median home price had climbed to historic highs.

Looking Forward

This narrative highlights the resilience of Georgia’s housing market. Despite downturns, long-term trends show significant appreciation. This story is critical for homebuyers, policymakers, and investors seeking to understand the market’s trajectory.
